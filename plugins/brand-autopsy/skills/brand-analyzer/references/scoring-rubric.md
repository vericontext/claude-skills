# Brand Health Scoring Rubric

Five dimensions of brand health, each scored 1-10. Use this rubric to assign consistent, defensible scores.

## Dimension 1: Clarity (Weight: 25%)

How clearly does the brand communicate what it does and for whom?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **9-10** | Instantly clear value proposition. A first-time visitor understands the product, audience, and benefit within 5 seconds. Hero headline is specific and compelling. No jargon without context. |
| **7-8** | Clear within 10 seconds. Value proposition is present but could be more specific. Minor ambiguity about target audience or exact offering. |
| **5-6** | Moderately clear. Requires scrolling or reading multiple sections to understand. Some vague or generic language ("We help businesses succeed"). |
| **3-4** | Confusing. Multiple messages compete. Value proposition is buried or abstract. Visitor would need to click around to understand. |
| **1-2** | Incomprehensible. No clear value proposition. Purpose of the site/product is unclear even after thorough reading. |

**Key signals:**
- Hero headline specificity
- Subheadline support
- "What we do" clarity without industry jargon
- Consistent messaging across pages

## Dimension 2: Consistency (Weight: 15%)

How consistent is the brand across visual and verbal elements?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **9-10** | Perfect consistency. Color palette strictly followed. Typography hierarchy clear. Tone of voice uniform. Visual style cohesive across all pages. |
| **7-8** | Very consistent with minor deviations. Perhaps one off-brand color or slight tone shift on a subpage. |
| **5-6** | Mostly consistent. Some visual inconsistencies (mixed button styles, varying spacing). Tone shifts between pages. |
| **3-4** | Noticeable inconsistency. Different visual styles on different pages. Mixed tone. Color palette seems unintentional. |
| **1-2** | No consistency. Feels like pages from different websites. No discernible brand system. |

**Key signals:**
- Color palette adherence (same colors across pages)
- Typography consistency (heading/body hierarchy)
- Button/CTA style uniformity
- Tone consistency (formal vs casual vs technical)
- Spacing and layout grid consistency

## Dimension 3: Differentiation (Weight: 25%)

How distinct is this brand from competitors in its space?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **9-10** | Unmistakably unique. Could not be confused with any competitor. Strong visual identity, unique voice, clear "only we do X" positioning. Category-defining. |
| **7-8** | Clearly differentiated. Has distinctive elements (voice, visual style, or positioning angle) but shares some patterns with the category. |
| **5-6** | Somewhat differentiated. Has a unique angle but visual/verbal execution is similar to competitors. "Better" rather than "different." |
| **3-4** | Weakly differentiated. Could swap logos with a competitor and few would notice. Generic category messaging. |
| **1-2** | Undifferentiated. Generic template feel. No unique visual or verbal identity. Commodity positioning. |

**Key signals:**
- Unique visual identity (not a generic template)
- Distinctive voice/tone
- Clear "only we" or "unlike competitors" positioning
- Memorable brand elements (name, colors, illustrations, mascot)
- Category-specific vs generic messaging

## Dimension 4: Trust (Weight: 20%)

How effectively does the brand build credibility and trust?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **9-10** | Comprehensive trust architecture. Customer logos from recognized brands. Specific testimonials with names/photos. Case studies with metrics. Security certifications. Press coverage. Large user numbers. |
| **7-8** | Strong trust signals. Has most of: logos, testimonials, numbers. Perhaps missing case studies or specific metrics. |
| **5-6** | Moderate trust signals. Some testimonials or logos present but not prominently placed. Generic "trusted by thousands" without specifics. |
| **3-4** | Weak trust signals. Few or no testimonials. No customer logos. No social proof numbers. |
| **1-2** | No trust signals. No social proof of any kind. New or unestablished brand with no credibility markers. |

**Key signals:**
- Customer/client logos (recognizable brands score higher)
- Testimonials with real names, photos, titles
- Specific numbers ("50,000+ teams" vs "many customers")
- Case studies with measurable outcomes
- Security/compliance certifications
- Press/media mentions
- Years in business or founding story

## Dimension 5: Conversion (Weight: 15%)

How effectively does the brand guide visitors toward action?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **9-10** | Optimized conversion path. Clear primary CTA above fold. Value-first approach (free trial/freemium). Friction-reducing elements (no credit card required). Strategic secondary CTAs. Clear pricing. |
| **7-8** | Good conversion design. Clear CTA present. Pricing available. Perhaps one friction point (required signup for pricing, long form). |
| **5-6** | Adequate conversion. CTA exists but not compelling. Pricing may be hidden. Multiple competing CTAs dilute focus. |
| **3-4** | Weak conversion. CTA is generic ("Learn More"). No clear next step. Pricing hidden behind "Contact Sales" without justification. |
| **1-2** | No conversion path. No clear CTA. Informational site with no action orientation. Visitor has no idea what to do next. |

**Key signals:**
- CTA prominence and clarity
- CTA text specificity ("Start Free Trial" > "Get Started" > "Learn More")
- Pricing transparency
- Friction reduction ("No credit card required", "Free forever plan")
- Funnel stage alignment
- Mobile CTA accessibility

## Overall Score Calculation

```
overall = (clarity * 0.25) + (differentiation * 0.25) + (trust * 0.20) + (consistency * 0.15) + (conversion * 0.15)
```

Round to one decimal place.

## Diagnosis Mapping

Based on overall score:
- **8.0-10.0**: "Excellent Brand Health" — Strong, well-positioned brand
- **6.0-7.9**: "Good Brand Health" — Solid foundation with room for improvement
- **4.0-5.9**: "Fair Brand Health" — Significant gaps need attention
- **2.0-3.9**: "Poor Brand Health" — Major overhaul recommended
- **1.0-1.9**: "Critical" — Brand fundamentals need to be established

## Prescriptions

Generate 3-5 prescriptions based on the lowest-scoring dimensions. Each prescription should be:
- **Actionable**: Specific enough to implement
- **Prioritized**: high/medium/low based on score gap and impact
- **Effort-estimated**: quick-win (< 1 week), moderate (1-4 weeks), major (1+ months)

Focus prescriptions on the 2-3 weakest dimensions. Don't prescribe improvements for dimensions already scoring 8+.
